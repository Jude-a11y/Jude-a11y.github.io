About me

I am a doctoral student specializing in psychological methods and diagnostics, with a focus on the assessment, prediction and intervention of mental health. In the last few years, my research explores how early experiences influence the development of positive attributes and behaviors. Now, I am engaged in utilizing mobile sensing and machine learning techniques to get digital phenotypings of mental health My goal is to leverage this research to help individuals enhance attributes that contribute to their well-being.

Research

As a student with a background in psychology and information sciences, I am passionate about leveraging information technology to enhance mental well-being. My research primarily centers on psychological methods and diagnostics which includes the study of mobile sensing, psychoinformatics, and related areas. My main research topics include:

  • Feature engineering on passively collected data of daily behavior.
  • Building digital profiles of mental health status with mobile sensing.
  • Designing systems that provide personalized intervention strategies.
